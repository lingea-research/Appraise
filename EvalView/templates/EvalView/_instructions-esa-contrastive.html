• Highlighting errors:
    • Select the part of translation where you have identified a translation error (drag or click start & end).
    • Click on the highlight to change error severity (minor/major) or remove the highlight.
    • If something is missing, highlight the word [MISSING] to mark this type of error.
  • Choosing error severity:
    • Minor errors: Style, grammar, word choice could be better or more natural.
    • Major errors:: The meaning is changed significantly and/or the part is really hard to understand.
  • Note:
    • Highlight the word or general area of the error and use multiple highlights for different errors.
    • Pay particular attention to translation consistency between texts across the whole document.
    • If the translation is in the wrong language, mark it fully and assign lowest score.
    • If the translation contains additional text not present in the source text or alternative secondary translation, mark it as a major error.

Rate the translations based on the marked errors and the following criteria:

Naturalness: Does the translation sound fluent in the target language?
Accuracy: Does the translation correctly preserve the meaning of the source text?
Coherence: Does the sentence translation fit well in the document context?
