Assess the translation quality on the 10-point scale using quality levels as follows:

  • Not acceptable (1-2): The translation is misleading or incomprehensible, with severe meaning errors and broken language that make it unusable.
  • Borderline (3-4): The text conveys some meaning but contains frequent errors and awkward phrasing; it requires heavy editing to be useful.
  • Acceptable (5-6): The message is clear and mostly accurate, though minor issues in grammar or style remain; suitable with light to moderate editing.
  • Good (7-8): The translation is accurate and fluent with only rare awkwardness; tone and style are appropriate, needing minimal polishing.
  • Excellent (9-10): The text reads like a native-level, publication-ready piece: fully accurate, idiomatic, and stylistically polished.
